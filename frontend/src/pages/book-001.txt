The Midnight Library
by Matt Haig

Between life and death there is a library.

Nora Seed stands on the threshold of her own existence, uncertain whether to continue. In this liminal space she discovers the Midnight Library — a place outside of time, filled with books that represent every life she could have lived.

With her cat gone and her dreams unfulfilled, Nora steps through the pages of possibility. Each book holds a different version of herself: the Olympic swimmer, the glaciologist in the Arctic, the rock star who never gave it all up.

But as she moves between lives, she begins to ask a deeper question: what does it mean to live a life well?

The shelves stretch endlessly in both directions. Every spine glows faintly in the half-light. Mrs Elm, her childhood librarian, sits quietly at the desk and smiles.

"Every book in this library is a life you could have lived," she says. "Some are very different from each other. Some are almost the same. The most important thing is that you choose."

Nora reaches for the first spine. Cool to the touch. She opens the cover.

The world dissolves into light.
