The Alchemist
by Paulo Coelho

The boy's name was Santiago.

He had a dream — twice — about a treasure buried near the Egyptian pyramids. A Gypsy woman told him it was real. Then an old king appeared in the marketplace and told him the same thing.

"When you want something," the king said, "all the universe conspires in helping you to achieve it."

Santiago sold his flock of sheep and set out across the Sahara.

The desert is not empty. It speaks. It tests. A caravan of strangers. A tribal war that forces a months-long stop. An alchemist who can turn lead to gold and who recognizes in Santiago a kindred spirit.

"The secret of life is to fall seven times and to get up eight times."

The Soul of the World connects all things. Love is the force that transforms and improves the Soul of the World. Every person who has a dream is capable of achieving it — but most turn back when the journey becomes difficult.

Santiago does not turn back.

He reaches the pyramids.

He finds what he was looking for — but not in the place he expected.

The treasure was never the point. The journey was.
