Atomic Habits
by James Clear

The aggregation of marginal gains.

In 1908, the British cycling team had never won a single gold medal. By 2012, they had dominated the Tour de France and the Olympics alike. The difference was not talent. It was a relentless focus on getting 1% better at everything.

Atomic Habits is a practical guide to building good habits and breaking bad ones. James Clear argues that we do not rise to the level of our goals — we fall to the level of our systems.

Four Laws of Behavior Change:
1. Make it obvious.
2. Make it attractive.
3. Make it easy.
4. Make it satisfying.

Habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. The same way that money multiplies through compound interest, the effects of your habits multiply as you repeat them.

You do not need to be the victim of your environment. You can also be the architect of it.

The most powerful outcomes are delayed. Time magnifies the margin between success and failure.

Get 1% better every day.
